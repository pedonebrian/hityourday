Members Only • Week 6

Head Movement

This week is about learning how to move your head with control, balance, and purpose. We’re focusing on slips, rolls, and small defensive movements that help you stay protected while staying ready to punch back.

30-Minute Session
Defense + Conditioning
Equipment: 1-3lb handweights

Theme of the Week

Head movement is not about big dramatic motion. It is about small, controlled movements that help you avoid punches while staying balanced. This week is about slipping, rolling, resetting, and learning how to move without losing your stance.

Main Focus

Move small. Stay balanced. Keep your eyes up. Return to guard.

Session Objective

By the end of this session, you should feel more comfortable slipping punches, rolling underneath, and adding simple head movement into your boxing combinations.

What to Remember

  • Keep your hands up while moving your head
  • Move from your knees, not just your waist
  • Keep your chin tucked
  • Do not over-move or lose your balance
  • Always come back ready to punch

Warmup • 5 Minutes

1 minute

Light bounce or jog in place

1 minute

Arm circles, shoulder rolls, and torso rotations

1 minute

Boxing stance resets with small knee bends

2 minutes with handweights

Light shadowboxing with slips after each punch

Technique Focus • Head Movement

Key Coaching Points

  • Start from a strong boxing stance
  • Slip by slightly moving your head off the center line
  • Roll by bending the knees and moving under the punch
  • Keep your eyes forward
  • Stay tight and return to guard after every movement

Common Mistakes

  • Bending too far at the waist
  • Dropping the hands while slipping
  • Moving too big and losing balance
  • Looking down during rolls
  • Forgetting to punch after defending

Main Training • 15 Minutes

Round 1 — Slip Left + Slip Right

Practice small slips side to side while staying in stance and keeping your guard up.

Round 2 — Jab + Slip

Throw a jab, then slip off the center line and reset.

Round 3 — Cross + Roll

Throw the cross, then bend the knees and roll underneath before returning to stance.

Round 4 — Slip + Lead Hook

Slip first, then come back with a controlled lead hook.

Round 5 — Freestyle Defense Round

Mix punches, slips, rolls, and resets while staying balanced and controlled.

Strength Block • 5 Minutes

  • 12 squats
  • 10 alternating reverse lunges
  • 20 standing twists
Complete 2 rounds with minimal rest.

Why the Strength Work Matters

  • Squats help you move from the legs during rolls
  • Lunges improve balance and stance control
  • Twists help connect defense with counterpunching
