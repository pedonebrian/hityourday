Members Only • Week 5

Lead Uppercut

This week is about developing a clean, tight lead uppercut. We’re focusing on bending the knees, driving from the legs, and keeping the punch compact. The goal is to generate power from underneath without lifting up or losing balance.

30-Minute Session
Technique + Conditioning
Equipment: 1-3lb handweights

Theme of the Week

The lead uppercut is a short, explosive punch that comes from underneath. It is not a big lifting motion — it’s a tight, controlled movement driven by your legs and core. This week is about staying compact and powerful.

Main Focus

Bend the knees. Stay tight. Drive up through the target. Return to guard.

Session Objective

By the end of this session, you should feel comfortable throwing a lead uppercut with balance, control, and proper mechanics from the legs.

What to Remember

  • Keep your elbow close to your body
  • Bend slightly at the knees before driving up
  • Do not lift your chin as you punch
  • Stay compact — no big swings
  • Bring the hand right back to guard

Warmup • 5 Minutes

1 minute

1 minute

2 minutes with handweights

Light shadowboxing with slow uppercuts focusing on form

Technique Focus • Lead Uppercut

Key Coaching Points

  • Start from a strong, balanced stance
  • Dip slightly with the knees — not the upper body
  • Drive up through the legs and core
  • Keep the punch short and tight
  • Opposite hand stays protecting your face

Common Mistakes

  • Lifting up too much or jumping
  • Dropping the opposite hand
  • Overextending the punch
  • Leaning forward instead of driving upward

Main Training • 15 Minutes

Round 1 — Single Lead Uppercut

Throw one uppercut at a time, focusing on clean form and reset.

Round 2 — Jab + Lead Uppercut

Use the jab to set distance, then follow with the uppercut.

Round 3 — Slip + Lead Uppercut

Add a small slip before throwing the uppercut.

Round 4 — Lead Uppercut + Hook

Throw the uppercut, then follow with a hook for flow.

Round 5 — Freestyle Uppercut Round

Mix all combinations while staying controlled and balanced.

Strength Block • 5 Minutes

  • 12 squats
  • 10 alternating lunges
  • 20 standing twists
Complete 2 rounds with minimal rest.

Why the Strength Work Matters

  • Squats build the leg drive for uppercuts
  • Lunges improve balance and control
  • Twists reinforce rotational power
