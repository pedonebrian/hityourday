Members Only • Week 10

Complex Combinations

This week is about connecting punches together with rhythm, balance, and control. Complex combinations teach you how to move from one punch to the next without rushing, losing stance, or throwing wildly.

30-Minute Session
Rhythm + Flow
Equipment: 1-3lb handweights

Theme of the Week

Complex combinations are longer punch sequences that challenge your timing, coordination, and conditioning. This week is about learning how to combine punches smoothly while staying balanced and relaxed.

Main Focus

Stay balanced. Build rhythm. Finish strong.

Session Objective

By the end of this session, you should feel more comfortable throwing longer combinations while keeping your stance, breathing, and control.

What to Remember

  • Do not rush the combination
  • Return each hand back to guard
  • Stay balanced between punches
  • Breathe with every punch
  • Finish the combo and reset your stance

Warmup • 5 Minutes

1 minute

Light shadowboxing with basic punches

1 minute

Jab + cross rhythm drill

1 minute

Hook + uppercut flow drill

2 minutes with handweights

Light shadowboxing with 3- and 4-punch combinations

Technique Focus • Complex Combinations

Key Coaching Points

  • Start slow before adding speed
  • Keep your feet underneath you
  • Rotate your hips and shoulders with each punch
  • Mix straight punches, hooks, and uppercuts
  • Reset your guard after the final punch

Common Mistakes

  • Throwing too fast too soon
  • Dropping hands during longer combinations
  • Standing still and stiff
  • Losing balance after hooks or uppercuts
  • Forgetting to breathe during the combo

Main Training • 15 Minutes

Round 1 — Jab + Cross + Lead Hook

Start with a simple 3-punch combination and focus on clean technique.

Round 2 — Jab + Cross + Lead Hook + Cross

Add the rear hand at the end and reset your stance after the final punch.

Round 3 — Jab + Cross + Lead Uppercut + Cross

Change levels with the uppercut, then finish straight down the middle.

Round 4 — Jab + Cross + Hook to the Body + Lead Hook

Work high to low, then come back upstairs with control.

Round 5 — Freestyle Combination Round

Mix 3-, 4-, and 5-punch combinations while staying balanced and relaxed.

Strength Block • 5 Minutes

  • 20 squat punches
  • 20 plank shoulder taps
  • 30 jumping jacks
Complete 2 rounds with minimal rest.

Why the Strength Work Matters

  • Squat punches build leg drive and combination endurance
  • Plank shoulder taps improve core control and shoulder stability
  • Jumping jacks improve rhythm, breathing, and conditioning

Coach’s Note

Complex combinations are not about throwing as fast as possible. They are about staying calm, building rhythm, and keeping control as the punches get longer. Smooth is fast. Balanced is powerful.
