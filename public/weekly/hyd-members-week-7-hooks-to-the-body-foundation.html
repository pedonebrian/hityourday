Members Only • Week 7

Hooks to the Body

This week is about learning how to throw powerful and controlled hooks to the body. We’re focusing on rotation, balance, level changes, and generating power without sacrificing technique.

30-Minute Session
Power + Technique
Equipment: 1-3lb handweights

Theme of the Week

Body hooks are powerful punches that attack underneath the guard. This week is about learning how to change levels, rotate through the hips, and stay balanced while throwing hooks to the body with control.

Main Focus

Bend the knees. Rotate the hips. Stay tight and balanced.

Session Objective

By the end of this session, you should feel more comfortable throwing lead and rear hooks to the body while staying balanced and protecting yourself.

What to Remember

  • Change levels with your legs, not your back
  • Rotate your hips for power
  • Keep the opposite hand up
  • Stay compact and controlled
  • Return to stance after every hook

Warmup • 5 Minutes

1 minute

Light bounce or jog in place

1 minute

Torso rotations and shoulder rolls

1 minute

Boxing stance resets with level changes

2 minutes with handweights

Light shadowboxing with hooks to the body

Technique Focus • Hooks to the Body

Key Coaching Points

  • Bend the knees slightly before throwing
  • Rotate through the hips and core
  • Keep the elbow bent at 90 degrees
  • Keep your eyes up while changing levels
  • Return quickly to your guard after punching

Common Mistakes

  • Dropping both hands while punching
  • Bending too far forward
  • Throwing arm punches without hip rotation
  • Standing too tall during body shots
  • Losing balance after the punch

Main Training • 15 Minutes

Round 1 — Lead Hook to the Body

Focus on level changes, hip rotation, and returning to guard.

Round 2 — Rear Hook to the Body

Rotate through the hips and stay balanced after every punch.

Round 3 — Jab + Lead Body Hook

Use the jab to create rhythm, then change levels for the hook.

Round 4 — Cross + Rear Body Hook

Focus on smooth rotation and staying compact.

Round 5 — Freestyle Body Punching Round

Mix jabs, crosses, hooks to the head, and hooks to the body while staying relaxed and controlled.

Strength Block • 5 Minutes

  • 15 squats
  • 20 standing twists
  • 10 pushups
Complete 2 rounds with minimal rest.

Why the Strength Work Matters

  • Squats improve level changes and leg drive
  • Standing twists strengthen rotational power
  • Pushups help build punching endurance and stability
