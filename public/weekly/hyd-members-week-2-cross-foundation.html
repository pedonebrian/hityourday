Members Only • Week 2

Cross Foundation

This week is about building a clean, strong cross. We’re focusing on rotation, balance, power from the ground up, and snapping the punch straight back home. We’ll also add a little strength work to help support power and stability.

30-Minute Session
Technique + Conditioning
Equipment: 1-3lb handweights

Theme of the Week

A sharp cross is one of your best power punches. It works best when it stays clean and direct. This week is about learning to rotate well, stay balanced, and throw the cross without overreaching.

Main Focus

Turn the hip. Turn the shoulder. Punch straight. Snap it back. Stay balanced.

Session Objective

By the end of this session, you should be able to throw a cleaner cross with proper rotation, better balance, and a quicker return to stance. You should feel where the power comes from instead of just pushing the punch out.

What to Remember

  • Rotate from the back foot, hip, and shoulder
  • Punch straight down the middle
  • Keep the lead hand up
  • Do not fall forward into the punch
  • Snap the cross back home right away

Warmup • 5 Minutes

1 minute

Light bounce, march, or jog in place

1 minute

Arm circles, torso twists, shoulder rolls, and loose upper-body movement

1 minute

Boxing stance resets — set feet, hands up, relax, rotate lightly

2 minutes with handweights

Easy shadowboxing using stance, guard, and light single crosses with clean return

Technique Focus • The Cross

Key Coaching Points

  • Start from a strong stance with your body under control
  • Drive from the back side — foot, hip, then shoulder
  • Let the rear shoulder protect the chin as you extend
  • Lead hand stays up and active
  • Return to stance immediately after every cross

Common Mistakes

  • Overthrowing and falling forward
  • Dropping the lead hand
  • Letting the cross loop instead of staying straight
  • Not rotating enough through the hips

Main Training • 15 Minutes

Round 1 — Single Cross

Throw single crosses with control. Reset after every punch. Focus on clean rotation, straight line, and quick return.

Round 2 — Double Cross Rhythm

Throw two controlled crosses with a reset in between. Stay smooth and balanced. Do not rush the second punch.

Round 3 — Jab + Cross

Start connecting the straight punches together. Use the jab to set the line, then rotate into the cross cleanly.

Round 4 — Cross + Step Back

Throw the cross, then immediately step back to reset your distance. Stay balanced after the punch and bring your stance back together.

Round 5 — Freestyle Straight Punch Round

Mix single crosses, jab + cross, and cross + step back. Keep the round centered around clean straight punches and staying relaxed.

Strength Block • 5 Minutes

Build support for rotation, power, posture, and stability.

  • 10 bodyweight squats
  • 10 alternating reverse lunges or deadlifts
  • 20 standing twists
Complete 2 rounds with good form and minimal rest.

Why the Strength Work Matters

  • Squats help build leg drive and balance
  • Lunges improve lower-body control and coordination
  • Standing twists support rotational awareness for the cross
