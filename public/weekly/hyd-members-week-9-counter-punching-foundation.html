Counter Punching

This week is about learning how to stay calm, defend, and respond. Counter punching teaches you how to make a simple defensive movement, create an opening, and immediately fire back with control.

30-Minute Session
Defense + Offense
Equipment: 1-3lb handweights

Theme of the Week

Counter punching is the art of responding after your opponent attacks. Instead of always throwing first, you defend, create an opening, and immediately answer back.

Main Focus

Defend. Respond. Reset.

Session Objective

By the end of this session, you should feel more comfortable making a defensive movement and immediately following it with a punch.

What to Remember

  • Defense creates offense
  • Keep your eyes forward
  • Stay balanced while defending
  • Counter immediately after movement
  • Keep your punches simple and sharp

Warmup • 5 Minutes

1 minute

Light shadowboxing

1 minute

Slip left and right

1 minute

Step back and reset

2 minutes with handweights

Light shadowboxing with slips and counters

Technique Focus • Counter Punching

Key Coaching Points

  • Stay relaxed during defense
  • Move your head just enough
  • Keep your hands up
  • Return fire immediately after defending
  • Stay balanced before, during, and after the counter

Common Mistakes

  • Moving too far when slipping
  • Dropping your hands
  • Waiting too long to counter
  • Leaning instead of moving
  • Throwing wild punches after defense

Main Training • 15 Minutes

Round 1 — Slip + Jab

Slip outside an imaginary punch and fire the jab.

Round 2 — Slip + Cross

Defend first, then immediately throw the rear hand.

Round 3 — Step Back + Cross

Create distance with your feet and counter with a straight cross.

Round 4 — Slip + Jab + Cross

Use a small defensive movement, then answer with a simple combination.

Round 5 — Freestyle Counter Round

Mix slips, step backs, footwork, and counters while staying relaxed.

Strength Block • 5 Minutes

  • 20 squat punches
  • 20 alternating reverse lunges
  • 30 high knees
Complete 2 rounds with minimal rest.

Why the Strength Work Matters

  • Squat punches build leg drive and punching control
  • Reverse lunges improve balance and stability
  • High knees improve foot speed, timing, and conditioning
