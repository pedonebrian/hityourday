Move of the Week: Step Over Pivot

This week starts our new Move of the Week format. Instead of focusing on one foundational skill, we’re locking down one complete boxing movement: jab with a step over, pivot to create the angle, lead hook, then double jab to exit and reset.

30-Minute Session
Move of the Week
Equipment: 1-3lb handweights

Theme of the Week

Now that we’ve built a foundation of punches, footwork, and combinations, this next phase is about learning complete boxing movements. The Step Over Pivot teaches you how to move off the center line, create an angle, land the hook, and exit safely behind the jab.

Main Focus

Create the angle. Land the hook. Exit under control.

Move of the Week

Step Over Pivot

1. Jab
2. Step Over
3. Pivot
4. Lead Hook
5. Double Jab Exit
Full Combination:
Jab + Step Over → Pivot → Lead Hook → Double Jab Exit

Session Objective

By the end of this session, you should feel more comfortable performing the Step Over Pivot smoothly without rushing, crossing your feet, or losing balance after the hook.

What to Remember

  • Use small steps
  • Keep your eyes forward
  • Do not cross your feet
  • Pivot before throwing the hook
  • Use the double jab to exit and reset

Why This Move Works

  • The jab keeps the opponent busy
  • The step over moves you off the center line
  • The pivot creates a new attacking angle
  • The lead hook lands from the side
  • The double jab helps you exit safely

Technique Breakdown • Step Over Pivot

Part 1 — Jab

Start with a sharp jab. Keep your rear hand up and stay balanced. The jab creates rhythm and sets up the movement.

Part 2 — Step Over

Take a small controlled step over to move off the center line. The step should create an angle without making you lose your stance.

Part 3 — Pivot

Pivot around your lead foot. Do not spin too far. The goal is to turn your body just enough to create a better punching angle.

Part 4 — Lead Hook

Throw the lead hook immediately after the pivot. Rotate through the hips and shoulders while keeping the opposite hand up.

Part 5 — Double Jab Exit

Use the double jab to move out, create space, and reset your stance. Finish balanced and ready to continue.

Common Mistakes

  • Crossing the feet during the step over
  • Spinning too far on the pivot
  • Throwing the hook before creating the angle
  • Dropping the opposite hand during the hook
  • Forgetting to exit after landing the hook

Main Training • 15 Minutes

Round 1 — Step Over Drill

Practice the step over slowly while staying balanced in your stance.

Round 2 — Pivot Drill

Work the pivot by itself. Keep your eyes forward and rotate under control.

Round 3 — Jab + Step Over + Pivot

Add the jab before the movement and focus on creating a clean angle.

Round 4 — Jab + Step Over + Pivot + Lead Hook

Throw the hook after the pivot and finish balanced.

Round 5 — Full Move of the Week

Jab + Step Over → Pivot → Lead Hook → Double Jab Exit.

Conditioning Block • 5 Minutes

  • 20 squat punches
  • 20 reverse lunges
  • 30 mountain climbers
Complete 2 rounds with minimal rest.

Why the Conditioning Matters

  • Squat punches build leg drive and punching rhythm
  • Reverse lunges improve balance and lower-body control
  • Mountain climbers build conditioning for movement under fatigue
