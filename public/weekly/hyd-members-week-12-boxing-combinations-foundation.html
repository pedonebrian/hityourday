Members Only • Week 12

Boxing Combinations

This week is about taking our boxing combinations one step further by adding movement, level changes, and sharper resets. We’re focusing on putting punches together while staying balanced, relaxed, and ready for the next action.

30-Minute Session
Combination Flow
Equipment: 1-3lb handweights

Theme of the Week

Strong boxing combinations require more than memorizing punches. They require rhythm, balance, breathing, and the ability to reset after every sequence. This week is about improving combination flow while adding movement and control.

Main Focus

Flow through the combo. Move with control. Reset with balance.

Session Objective

By the end of this session, you should feel more comfortable throwing longer combinations while adding small movements, level changes, and clean resets after each sequence.

What to Remember

  • Stay relaxed through the whole combination
  • Keep your feet active but controlled
  • Change levels with your legs, not your back
  • Breathe out with every punch
  • Finish every combination in stance

Warmup • 5 Minutes

1 minute

Light bounce with jab + cross

1 minute

Step + jab, then reset stance

1 minute

Jab + cross + hook rhythm drill

2 minutes with handweights

Light shadowboxing using movement and 3- to 5-punch combinations

Technique Focus • Combination Flow

Key Coaching Points

  • Build combinations with rhythm, not tension
  • Use small steps before or after the combination
  • Rotate your hips and shoulders on every punch
  • Mix head shots, body shots, hooks, and uppercuts
  • Reset your stance before starting the next combination

Common Mistakes

  • Trying to throw every punch at full speed
  • Forgetting footwork during the combination
  • Dropping hands after body shots
  • Leaning forward instead of changing levels properly
  • Finishing off balance after the last punch

Main Training • 15 Minutes

Round 1 — Step + Jab + Cross + Lead Hook

Use a small step to enter, then throw the combination with balance.

Round 2 — Jab + Cross + Lead Hook + Step Back

Finish the combination, step back, and reset your stance.

Round 3 — Jab + Cross + Body Hook + Cross

Change levels for the body shot, then come back straight down the middle.

Round 4 — Jab + Lead Uppercut + Cross + Lead Hook

Work different punch angles while keeping your feet underneath you.

Round 5 — Freestyle Combination + Movement Round

Use 3-, 4-, and 5-punch combinations with small steps, resets, and level changes.

Strength Block • 5 Minutes

  • 20 reverse lunges
  • 20 squat punches
  • 30 mountain climbers
Complete 2 rounds with minimal rest.

Why the Strength Work Matters

  • Reverse lunges build balance and leg control
  • Squat punches connect lower-body strength to punching rhythm
  • Mountain climbers improve conditioning for longer combinations
