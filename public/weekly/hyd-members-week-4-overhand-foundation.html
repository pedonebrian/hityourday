Members Only • Week 4

Overhand

This week is about learning how to throw the overhand with control, balance, and purpose. We’re focusing on turning the rear side, keeping the punch tight, and avoiding the common mistake of winding up too big. We’ll also include a small strength block to support rotation, legs, and core stability.

30-Minute Session
Technique + Conditioning
Equipment: 1-3lb handweights

Theme of the Week

The overhand is a powerful punch that comes over the top, but it still needs structure. This week is about learning how to throw it without losing balance, dropping your guard, or swinging wildly. The goal is controlled power.

Main Focus

Turn the rear side. Keep it compact. Chin down. Hand returns home.

Session Objective

By the end of this session, you should feel more comfortable throwing an overhand with clean rotation, better balance, and a quick return to guard. The goal is to make the punch powerful without making it reckless.

What to Remember

  • Start from a strong stance
  • Turn the rear hip and shoulder through the punch
  • Keep your chin tucked as the punch comes over
  • Do not wind up or swing too wide
  • Return right back to guard after every rep

1 minute

2 minutes with handweights

Easy shadowboxing with guard, light jabs, and slow overhands focusing on form

Technique Focus • The Overhand

Key Coaching Points

  • Start balanced with both hands up
  • Use the rear foot, hip, and shoulder to drive the punch
  • Let the punch come slightly over the top, not straight down
  • Keep the opposite hand protecting your face
  • Recover back to stance immediately after throwing

Common Mistakes

  • Winding up before throwing
  • Leaning too far forward
  • Dropping the lead hand
  • Throwing with only the arm instead of rotating the body

Main Training • 15 Minutes

Round 1 — Single Overhand

Throw one overhand at a time with control. Reset after every rep. Focus on turning the rear side and bringing the hand back home.

Round 2 — Jab + Overhand

Use the jab to set the line, then bring the overhand over the top. Stay balanced and avoid reaching.

Round 3 — Slip + Overhand

Add a small slip before throwing the overhand. Keep the movement tight and controlled, then return to stance.

Round 4 — Overhand + Step Back

Throw the overhand, then step back and reset. Focus on not falling forward after the punch.

Round 5 — Freestyle Overhand Round

Mix single overhands, jab + overhand, slip + overhand, and overhand + step back. Keep everything sharp, balanced, and controlled.

Strength Block • 5 Minutes

Build support for rotation, legs, and core control.

  • 10 bodyweight squats
  • 10 alternating reverse lunges or deadlifts
  • 20 standing cross-body twists
Complete 2 rounds with good form and minimal rest.

Why the Strength Work Matters

  • Squats help build balance and lower-body drive
  • Reverse lunges improve control and stance stability
  • Cross-body twists reinforce the rotation needed for the overhand
