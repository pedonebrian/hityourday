Lead Hook

This week is about building a clean, sharp lead hook. We’re focusing on balance, elbow position, rotation, and learning how to turn the punch over without swinging too wide. We’ll also keep a small strength block to help support power and control.

30-Minute Session
Technique + Conditioning
Equipment: 1-3lb handweights

Theme of the Week

The lead hook is one of the most powerful punches in boxing when it stays short, tight, and balanced. This week is about learning how to rotate into the hook, keep the punch compact, and return to stance without getting pulled off balance.

Main Focus

Stay tight. Turn the hip. Let the hook travel short. Elbow up. Snap it back home.

Session Objective

By the end of this session, you should feel more comfortable throwing a compact lead hook with control, better body rotation, and a quicker return to guard. The goal is to stop arm-swinging and start turning the punch correctly.

What to Remember

  • Keep the hook short and tight
  • Turn through the lead side with control
  • Do not let the punch get long or loopy
  • Keep your opposite hand up
  • Return right back to stance after every rep

Technique Focus • The Lead Hook

Key Coaching Points

  • Start from a balanced stance with your weight under you
  • Turn the lead foot, hip, and shoulder together
  • Keep the hook compact — think short and sharp
  • Keep the elbow lifted in line with the punch
  • Bring it right back to guard after every hook

Common Mistakes

  • Swinging too wide with the arm
  • Dropping the opposite hand
  • Leaning too far and losing balance
  • Throwing only with the arm instead of turning the body

Main Training • 15 Minutes

Round 1 — Single Lead Hook

Throw one lead hook at a time with control. Reset after every rep. Stay focused on a short path, solid rotation, and quick return.

Round 2 — Double Hook Rhythm

Throw two controlled lead hooks with a reset between. Stay balanced and avoid letting the second punch get sloppy or wide.

Round 3 — Jab + Lead Hook

Use the jab to set the line, then turn into the lead hook. Think smooth rhythm and clean transitions between straight and curved punches.

Round 4 — Lead Hook + Step Back

Throw the hook, then immediately step back and reset your stance. Focus on staying composed after the punch instead of hanging in place.

Round 5 — Freestyle Hook Round

Mix single lead hooks, jab + hook, and hook + step back. Keep the round centered around clean mechanics, balance, and staying relaxed.

Strength Block • 5 Minutes

Build support for rotation, leg strength, and upper-body control.

  • 10 bodyweight squats
  • 10 alternating reverse lunges or deadlifts
  • 20 standing torso twists
Complete 2 rounds with good form and minimal rest.

Why the Strength Work Matters

  • Squats help build balance and lower-body drive
  • Reverse lunges improve control and stance stability
  • Standing twists reinforce the turning motion needed for the hook

Coach’s Note
