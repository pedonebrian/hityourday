Members Only • Week 11

Boxing Combinations

This week is about sharpening our boxing combinations with more purpose, rhythm, and control. We’re focusing on connecting punches together, changing levels, and learning how to finish each combination balanced and ready to move again.

30-Minute Session
Combination Work
Equipment: 1-3lb handweights

Theme of the Week

Boxing combinations are more than just throwing multiple punches. They teach rhythm, balance, timing, and control. This week is about building clean combinations that flow together while keeping strong boxing fundamentals.

Main Focus

Punch with purpose. Stay balanced. Reset after every combo.

Session Objective

By the end of this session, you should feel more comfortable throwing boxing combinations with better rhythm, cleaner transitions, and more control between each punch.

What to Remember

  • Start slow before adding speed
  • Bring every punch back to guard
  • Stay balanced through the whole combination
  • Use your hips and shoulders, not just your arms
  • Finish the combo, then reset your stance

Warmup • 5 Minutes

1 minute

Light bounce with basic punches

1 minute

Jab + cross rhythm drill

1 minute

Jab + cross + hook flow drill

2 minutes with handweights

Light shadowboxing using 3- and 4-punch combinations

Technique Focus • Boxing Combinations

Key Coaching Points

  • Build the combination one punch at a time
  • Keep your feet underneath you
  • Breathe out with every punch
  • Mix straight punches, hooks, uppercuts, and body shots
  • End each combination in a strong boxing stance

Common Mistakes

  • Rushing through the combination
  • Dropping the opposite hand while punching
  • Throwing only with the arms
  • Leaning forward or losing balance
  • Forgetting to reset after the final punch

Main Training • 15 Minutes

Round 1 — Jab + Cross + Lead Hook

Focus on clean rhythm, full rotation, and returning each hand to guard.

Round 2 — Jab + Cross + Lead Hook + Cross

Add the rear hand at the end and finish balanced in your stance.

Round 3 — Jab + Cross + Lead Uppercut + Rear Hook

Work on changing punch angles while staying relaxed and controlled.

Round 4 — Jab + Cross + Lead Hook to the Body + Lead Hook

Go high, attack the body, then come back upstairs with control.

Round 5 — Freestyle Combination Round

Use 3-, 4-, and 5-punch combinations while focusing on balance, breathing, and clean resets.

Strength Block • 5 Minutes

  • 20 squat punches
  • 20 plank shoulder taps
  • 30 mountain climbers
Complete 2 rounds with minimal rest.

Why the Strength Work Matters

  • Squat punches build leg drive and punching endurance
  • Plank shoulder taps improve core control and shoulder stability
  • Mountain climbers build conditioning for longer combinations
