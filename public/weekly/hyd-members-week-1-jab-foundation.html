Members Only • Week 1

Jab Foundation

This week is about building the most important punch in boxing: the jab. We’re focusing on balance, clean mechanics, fast return, and a little bit of strength work to support better movement and control.

30-Minute Session
Technique + Conditioning
Equipment: 1-3lb handweights

Theme of the Week

A strong jab gives you rhythm, distance control, and confidence. This is where we start. If your jab improves, everything else gets better.

Main Focus

Stay balanced. Snap the jab. Bring it back fast. Do not reach. Do not lean.

Session Objective

By the end of this session, you should be able to throw a cleaner jab from stance, reset quickly, and stay more in control of your body while moving. Also switch and work the jab from both sides.

What to Remember

  • Hands up and relaxed
  • Punch straight out, not looping
  • Turn the fist over at the end
  • Bring the hand right back home
  • Other hand pressed to the face

Warmup • 5 Minutes

1 minute

Light bounce, march, or jog in place

1 minute

Arm circles, shoulder rolls, and loose upper-body movement

1 minute

Boxing stance resets — step in, set feet, hands up, relax

2 minutes with handweights

Easy shadowboxing using only stance, guard, and light single jabs

Technique Focus • The Jab

Key Coaching Points

  • Start from your stance, not from a reach
  • Push lightly from the ground and let the jab travel straight
  • Shoulder protects the chin as the punch extends
  • Rear hand stays home
  • Return to stance immediately after every punch

Common Mistakes

  • Dropping the back hand
  • Leaning too far forward
  • Pushing the punch instead of snapping it
  • Leaving the jab hanging out too long

Main Training • 15 Minutes

Round 1 — Single Jab

Throw single jabs with control. Reset after every punch. Focus on straight line, balance, and bringing the hand back fast.

Round 2 — Double Jab

Throw two clean jabs without rushing. Stay under control. Same mechanics, same balance, same return.

Round 3 — Jab + Step Back

Throw the jab, then immediately step back to reset your distance. This helps build control and ring awareness.

Round 4 — Jab While Moving

Small steps only. Move, set, jab. Move, set, jab. Do not cross your feet. Keep it smooth, not rushed.

Round 5 — Freestyle Jab Round

Mix single jabs, double jabs, and jab + step back. Keep the whole round centered around the jab and staying relaxed.

Strength Block • 5 Minutes

Build support for posture, shoulders, core, and legs.

  • 10 squats
  • 10 lunges (5 each side)
Complete 2 rounds with good form and minimal rest.

Why the Strength Work Matters

  • Squats help with balance and leg drive
  • Lunges improve coordination and movement

Live Session Plan

In the live session we’ll clean up stance, jab mechanics, and movement. Expect real-time corrections, simple reps, and a focused 30-minute class.

Homework

Do 3 short shadowboxing rounds before next class using only:

  • single jab
  • double jab
  • jab + step back

Coach’s Note

The goal this week is not to look flashy. The goal is to become sharper, cleaner, and more controlled. Master the basics and everything builds from there.
